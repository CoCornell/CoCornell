Co-Cornell is a team/project management system, which aims at helping team members keep track of the project. It can facilitate collaborations, handle accessibilities of different parts of the project, make notifications and announcements more visible, and thus, help members organize the project visually and scientifically.
In this application, a project will be abstracted as a board, and users can invite and add other members to this board, say, project. As the project can be separated into several parts/tasks, the board will be separated into corresponding lists. Each list may only be accessed by certain members, or by the whole team, which could be configured by the task manager. Team members who have access to the list can add card to the list. Cards can be all kinds of announcements that post to members, such as to-do list, known bugs, issue tracking, wiki, etc.
Team members can also upload pictures. Images can be processed by filters designed in the system. The filters associated with image processing, machine learning and pattern recognition will be implemented to improve inferior-quality images to make them easier to recognize. Also, OCR (Optical Character Recognition) will be applied to convert images of typed, or printed text into machine-encoded text. Compared with images, text can be edited more conveniently and easier to read when displayed on devices.
